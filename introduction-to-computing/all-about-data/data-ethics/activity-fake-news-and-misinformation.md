# Activity: Fake News and Misinformation

Part of computing and data science is making sure we use and share information in an ethical manner. Unfortunately, there are some bad actors out there which like to stoke outrage and use fear to gain followers and prestige. This can result in well meaning bystanders and followers in their networks sharing things that may _**feel**_ true, but are not always based in reality.

## The Bad News Game

Now, play the Fake News game and see how many followers you can obtain! How far will you go to become viral?

{% embed url="https://www.getbadnews.com/" %}

#### **Class Discussion:**

> What did you notice about the techniques used to spread misinformation in the game?
>
> How did it feel to “play as the bad guy” creating fake news?
>
> Which strategy (e.g., emotional language, fake experts, conspiracy) do you think is most convincing, and why?
>
> How can recognising these strategies help you in real life when you see news online?
>
> What will you do differently next time you read or share something online?

#### Activity: 100-200 Word Reflection

> After playing the _Bad News Game_ and discussing as a class, write a short reflection (100–200 words). In your response, you should:
>
> * Describe what you learned about how fake news is created and spread.
> * Explain how it felt to use the techniques of misinformation in the game.
> * Reflect on how you can use this experience to be more critical of news and social media in real life.

# SEE Model

Throughout high school you will be introduced to many ways of structuring your responses. In computing and engineering, we are a big fan of the **SEE Model.**

The **SEE Model** stands for <mark style="color:$danger;background-color:red;">**State**</mark>**,&#x20;**<mark style="color:blue;background-color:blue;">**Explain**</mark>**,&#x20;**<mark style="color:$success;background-color:green;">**Example**</mark>**.** It provides a simple guide for how to answer questions.

* <mark style="color:$danger;background-color:$danger;">**State:**</mark> <mark style="color:$danger;background-color:$danger;"></mark><mark style="color:$danger;background-color:$danger;">Start with a</mark> <mark style="color:$danger;background-color:$danger;"></mark><mark style="color:$danger;background-color:$danger;">**Statement**</mark><mark style="color:$danger;background-color:$danger;">. Clearly state the concept or idea you're trying to communicate. Make sure it's brief and to the point, and ensure it addresses the question.</mark>
* <mark style="color:blue;background-color:blue;">**Explain:**</mark> <mark style="color:blue;background-color:blue;"></mark><mark style="color:blue;background-color:blue;">Next, elaborate on that statement.</mark> <mark style="color:blue;background-color:blue;"></mark><mark style="color:blue;background-color:blue;">**Explain**</mark> <mark style="color:blue;background-color:blue;"></mark><mark style="color:blue;background-color:blue;">it in your own words, as if you're talking to a friend who's unfamiliar with the topic.</mark>
* <mark style="color:green;background-color:green;">**Example:**</mark> <mark style="color:green;background-color:green;"></mark><mark style="color:green;background-color:green;">Provide an</mark> <mark style="color:green;background-color:green;"></mark><mark style="color:green;background-color:green;">**Example.**</mark> <mark style="color:green;background-color:green;"></mark><mark style="color:green;background-color:green;">This is where you bring your statement to life. Use data and/or a real-world situation to support your point.</mark>



For instance, consider the question...

> Describe a desktop computer.

#### Answer:

<mark style="color:$danger;background-color:red;">A desktop computer is a personal computer that is used while sitting at a desk.</mark> <mark style="color:blue;background-color:blue;">It normally has separate parts, including a monitor, keyboard, mouse, and a main unit (the tower) that holds the important hardware. Unlike laptops, desktop computers are not easy to move around, but they are usually more powerful and can be upgraded.</mark> <mark style="color:green;background-color:green;">For example, in many schools, desktop computers are set up in computer labs so students can type assignments, make presentations, or use the internet.</mark>

The opening sentence **states** what a desktop computer is in simple terms. The next two sentences **explain** further, providing characteristics and features of a desktop computer when compared with a laptop (something most are more familiar with). The final sentence provides an **example** of where they might be used.
